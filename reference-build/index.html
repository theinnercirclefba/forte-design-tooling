The Club

An Edwardian heathland
that the great architects rebuilt.

Sherwood Forest is one of England's better-kept secrets — a top-100 heathland sitting on the southern edge of the ancient royal forest, designed by Harry Colt and finished by James Braid, with bunker refinements stretching across a hundred years of stewardship.

The club was founded as Mansfield Golf Club in 1895 and moved to the present site in 1912. Harry Colt — arguably the most influential British golf-course architect of his era — laid out the 1911 course. James Braid, five-time Open Champion, returned in 1927 to extend it to championship length. Henry Cotton, Frank Pennink, Donald Steel and the Hawtree practice have all touched the bunkering since.

The result is a course that demands strategic golf rather than length. Ninety-eight bunkers across heathland fairways. No water hazards. The famous “Amen Corner” — holes eleven through fourteen — sorts out the round more reliably than the scorecard would suggest.

The club is one of the four founding members of the Notts Heathland Tour, alongside Hollinwell, Coxmoor, and Lindrick. It hosts the Open Championship Regional Qualifying every year, and was named Tournament Venue of the Year by England Golf in 2023.
